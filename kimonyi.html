Eric is currently a fourth-year student at the Technical University of Kenya studying a Bachelor degree in Commerce, Bcom (Finance major).

Eric is a class-group leader tasked with the responsibility of mobilizing his fellow colleagues into doing class assignments and presentations.

Under his leadership, his group has always scored the highest marks during class academic paper-presentation, showcasing a higher level of organization and team spirit.

Eric was recently elected as the vice president of the Association of Business Students, ABS, at the University due to his persistence and continuous effort in seeking to create a productive collaboration between the association and the corporate world.

He is frequently selected to represent the association in various workshops and platforms.

He is also an active member of the Finance Students Association, FISA.

Besides his course work, Eric has done a couple of other courses such as a Diploma course in Project Management and a certificate in French.

Eric is a peer mentor, trained by various organizations on issues of academic and educational excellence, personal branding, value-based leadership and capacity building. He is a mentee, and an associate of AKAD Education Group-Africa (www.akadeducationafrica.com), an organization that serves as an educational placement consultancy to top academic institutions in Africa, USA and UK and focuses on capacity building, career training, mentorship and leadership excellence training to schools and universities.

Recently, Eric has had the privilege of being selected out of hundreds of able trainers across the country to work as a facilitator and peer mentor in Machakos and Kitui county with Plan International (www.planinternational.org) .During this training, Eric was able to add value to over 900 girls and 600 boys from over 60 schools in both counties.

Additionally, Eric has been able to share the same value on national TV stations, KTN and KBC talking to thousands of youth across East Africa and beyond on issues pertaining mentorship and education respectively.

Eric also has been able to train engineering and economics students from the University of Nairobi and The Technical University of Kenya on overseas postgraduate opportunities, in collaboration with the Graduate Management Admission Council (GMAC) on the value of the Graduate Management Admissions Test (GMAT).

Eric also gives motivational talks on academic and careers success to high schools; his former high school (Lenana School) and others including The Kenya High School and The Alliance High School during the schools career days and academics seminars.

Eric was the best pupil his primary school (Kwa-Ngumu Primary) and the third best pupil in his district (Mukaa) in 2008 when he did his KCPE, scoring a total of 408 marks out of the possible 500 leading to admission to the top national school, Lenana School.

During his high school, Eric demonstrated a great passion for leadership and education and was entrusted with several leadership positions in his third and fourth year;

  • House Captain,
  • Ushers chairperson,
  • Peer-educators representative
  • the French Clubs vice chairperson
promoting the moral and the spiritual life of the Lenana School Community.
